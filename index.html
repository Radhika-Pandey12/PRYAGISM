Prayagraj

{pra-yaga-raj} ~ noun

The city plays an iconic role both in Indian history and Hindu mythology. Along with that, Prayagraj is a humble abode with places where you can eat to your heart’s content.

Hello there!!

Allahabad, officially known as Prayagraj, and also known as Ilahabad and Prayag, is a metropolis in the Indian state of Uttar Pradesh.Allahabad lies close to Triveni Sangam, the "three-river confluence" of the Ganges, Yamuna and Sarasvati rivers.[1] It plays a central role in Hindu scriptures.

‘Sangam’ or river confluence is the point where two Indian holy rivers i.e. the Yamuna and the Ganges meet a mythological river of Hinduism called the Saraswati.As a matter of fact, Sangam looks spectacular regardless of the time you visit it.

A visit to Khusru Bagh must find a place on your list of things to do in Allahabad. Having three notable Mughal tombs, Khusru Bagh is surrounded by massive walls. One of the tombs is of the first son of Jehangir, Prince Khusru.

Being one of the things Allahabad is famous for, the Kumbh Mela is a Hindu pilgrimage in which pilgrims gather to bathe in the holy river. The Ardh Kumbh Mela takes place every 6 years, drawing interest from people around the world.

kumbh Sukrit

‘Kumbh’—its legacy is as impactful as its name. There could not be a more grandiose display of the religious faith and sentiments of Hindus than this, where multitudes of believers gather to bathe in the holy river, for a span of 50 days.
